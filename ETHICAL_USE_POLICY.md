# Bijmantra Ethical Use Policy

## Prohibition on Genetic Use Restriction Technologies (GURTs)

**Version 1.0 | Effective Date: December 4, 2025**

---

## PREAMBLE

Bijmantra is built on the foundational belief that plant breeding should serve humanity's collective interest in food security, agricultural sustainability, and farmer autonomy. This Ethical Use Policy establishes binding restrictions on the use of this software to ensure it is never employed in ways that undermine these principles.

The Creator, Denish Dholaria / R.E.E.V.A.i, hereby establishes this policy as an integral and inseparable component of the Bijmantra Open Source License with Attribution (BOSLA).

## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Genetic Use Restriction Technology" (GURT)** means any genetic engineering technique, method, process, or technology designed to:

   (a) Render seeds, propagules, or reproductive material sterile, non-viable, or incapable of germination without external intervention;
   
   (b) Require the application of proprietary chemical inducers, activators, or treatments to enable seed germination, plant growth, or reproduction;
   
   (c) Genetically program plants to produce non-viable offspring;
   
   (d) Restrict the inherent reproductive capacity of plants through genetic modification.

**1.2 "Terminator Technology"** (also known as "V-GURT" or Variety-level GURT) means any technology that produces sterile seeds, preventing farmers from saving and replanting seeds from their harvest.

**1.3 "Traitor Technology"** (also known as "T-GURT" or Trait-specific GURT) means any technology that requires chemical application to activate or maintain specific genetic traits in plants.

**1.4 "Seed Termination Technology"** means any method, whether genetic, chemical, biological, or technological, that prevents seeds from being saved, replanted, or used for subsequent generations of cultivation.

**1.5 "Prohibited Technologies"** collectively refers to all GURTs, Terminator Technology, Traitor Technology, Seed Termination Technology, and any functionally equivalent technologies regardless of nomenclature.

**1.6 "Breeding Activities"** means any activity involving the development, selection, crossing, modification, or improvement of plant varieties, cultivars, hybrids, lines, or populations.

## ARTICLE 2: ABSOLUTE PROHIBITION

**2.1 Prohibited Uses**

The use of Bijmantra software, in whole or in part, is STRICTLY PROHIBITED for:

   (a) The development, research, testing, or commercialization of any plant variety, hybrid, cultivar, line, or population incorporating Prohibited Technologies;
   
   (b) Managing, tracking, or facilitating breeding programs whose objective includes the incorporation of Prohibited Technologies;
   
   (c) Storing, analyzing, or processing data related to the development of Prohibited Technologies;
   
   (d) Any activity that directly or indirectly supports, enables, or contributes to the creation or deployment of Prohibited Technologies;
   
   (e) Breeding activities intended to produce varieties that restrict farmers' rights to save, exchange, or replant seeds.

**2.2 No Exceptions**

This prohibition is absolute and admits no exceptions. It applies regardless of:

   (a) The stated purpose or intent of the user;
   
   (b) Whether the Prohibited Technology is the primary or secondary objective;
   
   (c) The geographic location of the user or breeding activity;
   
   (d) The legal status of Prohibited Technologies in any jurisdiction;
   
   (e) Any commercial, research, or governmental justification offered.

## ARTICLE 4: RATIONALE AND PRINCIPLES

**4.1 Food Security**

Prohibited Technologies threaten global food security by:

   (a) Creating dependency on seed corporations for annual seed purchases;
   
   (b) Eliminating farmers' ability to adapt varieties to local conditions through selection;
   
   (c) Concentrating control of the food supply in corporate hands;
   
   (d) Increasing vulnerability of agricultural systems to supply chain disruptions.

**4.2 Farmer Rights and Autonomy**

The right of farmers to save, use, exchange, and sell farm-saved seed is recognized as fundamental by:

   (a) The International Treaty on Plant Genetic Resources for Food and Agriculture (ITPGRFA);
   
   (b) The United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Peasants (UNDROP);
   
   (c) Traditional agricultural practices spanning millennia;
   
   (d) The principle of seed sovereignty.

**4.3 Biodiversity Conservation**

Prohibited Technologies pose risks to biodiversity by:

   (a) Potentially contaminating non-modified crops through gene flow;
   
   (b) Reducing genetic diversity in agricultural systems;
   
   (c) Disrupting traditional seed-saving practices that maintain landrace diversity;
   
   (d) Creating unknown ecological risks from sterility genes in the environment.

**4.4 Ethical Plant Breeding**

Bijmantra is designed to support plant breeding that:

   (a) Serves the public good and farmer welfare;
   
   (b) Respects the inherent reproductive nature of plants;
   
   (c) Contributes to sustainable and resilient agricultural systems;
   
   (d) Honors the millennia of farmer innovation that created our crop diversity.
