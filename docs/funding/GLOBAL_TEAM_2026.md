## Executive Summary

BijMantra preview-1 represents 18+ months of solo development ‚Äî built in scraps of time, pushed forward one small step each day, funded by nothing but conviction.

To achieve its mission of unifying fragmented agricultural knowledge through AI-assisted cross-domain reasoning, the project must transition from a solo effort to a **globally distributed team**.

This document outlines the strategy for building that team in 2026.

### A Note on Reality

**We have no money to hire anyone.**

This is not a well-funded startup looking for contractors. This is an open source project built on borrowed time and stubborn hope. Every contributor who joins does so because they believe in the mission, not because there's a paycheck waiting.

If you're looking for compensation, we cannot offer it today. What we can offer:
- A platform that matters
- A community that values your work
- Recognition that lasts
- The satisfaction of building something real

> *"The measure of a life is not what we accumulate, but what we contribute. Plant every seed you carry."*

This project exists because one person refused to wait for permission or funding. It will grow because others choose to do the same.

### Why Global?

| Reason | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Climate Change is Global** | Solutions must work across climates, crops, and contexts |
| **Agricultural Knowledge is Distributed** | No single country holds all expertise |
| **24/7 Development** | Time zones enable continuous progress |
| **Diverse Perspectives** | Cross-cultural insights strengthen cross-domain reasoning |
| **Local Adaptation** | Regional experts ensure local relevance |

### 2026 Goal

**Build a core team of 15-25 contributors across 10+ countries, spanning 8+ disciplines.**

### 3.2 Governance Council

**Composition:** 3-5 members including:
- Project founder (permanent seat)
- 2-3 elected representatives (1-year terms)
- 1 external advisor (optional, non-voting)

**Responsibilities:**
- Strategic direction and roadmap
- Conflict resolution
- Partnership and funding decisions
- Code of Conduct enforcement

**Election Process:**
- Annual elections in December
- All Core Contributors eligible to vote
- Candidates must have 6+ months of contribution history

### 3.3 Domain Lead Selection

**Criteria:**
- Demonstrated expertise in domain
- 3+ months of active contribution
- Commitment to cross-domain philosophy
- Ability to mentor others

**Selection Process:**
- Nomination by Governance Council or self-nomination
- Review of contribution history
- Interview with existing leads
- Consensus approval

#### Language Policy

**Primary language: English**

This is not cultural preference ‚Äî it's practical necessity for global collaboration. English is the common language of:
- Open source development
- Scientific publishing
- International agricultural research
- The internet

All official communication (code, docs, Discord, meetings) should be in English. This ensures:
- Everyone can participate in every conversation
- No one is excluded from decisions
- Knowledge is accessible to all contributors

**That said:** We welcome contributors whose English isn't perfect. Clarity matters more than grammar. If you can communicate your ideas, you belong here.

**Regional channels:** As the community grows, we may add language-specific channels (e.g., `#spanish`, `#hindi`) for casual conversation and local support. But official project communication remains in English.

### 5.3 Async-First Principles

Given global distribution, we prioritize asynchronous communication:

1. **Document decisions** ‚Äî All significant decisions in GitHub Discussions
2. **Record meetings** ‚Äî All meetings recorded and summarized
3. **Overlap windows** ‚Äî Identify 2-hour daily overlap for real-time needs
4. **Response expectations** ‚Äî 24-48 hours for non-urgent matters
5. **No FOMO** ‚Äî Summaries ensure no one misses critical updates

| **Funding gaps** | High | Medium | Volunteer-first model, grant applications |

### 8.2 Contingency Plans

**If recruitment is slow:**
- Increase bounty amounts
- Partner with universities for course projects
- Engage professional recruiters for key roles

**If quality suffers:**
- Slow down, focus on fewer contributors
- Increase review requirements
- Mandatory mentorship for all new contributors

**If founder unavailable:**
- Governance Council assumes leadership
- Domain Leads maintain modules
- Community continues with documented processes
